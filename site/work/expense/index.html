Expense Tracker

Nothing brings two people together like camping, rock climbing, and writing your own budgeting software.

{% comment %}
{% endcomment %}

Problem

As a young couple fresh out of college, we were anxious to start on the right foot financially. We wanted to track our spending, but it was annoying to keep receipts and then remember to enter them into the computer at home. We wanted a solution that would move the data entry closer to the time of purchase. Besides, hooking up Perl and PostgreSQL just sounded fun.

Solution

Since mint.com wouldn't exist for another decade, we built a web-based expense tracker so we could enter expenditures from our desk computers at work. The interface was simple but functional: one screen for entering and categorizing purchases, and another for generating reports. We have fond memories of our first collaboration on a web project, even if we're quite happy that automated budgeting replaced it eventually.
