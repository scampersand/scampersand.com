Helping small businesses advertise on Facebook

Problem

Big companies are advertising on Facebook but small businesses aren't. It's not hard to understand why. For someone who isn't a marketing expert, it's not obvious how to put together an ad that will capture the interest of viewers. Then there's the Facebook ads manager, a complex set of controls for budgeting, scheduling and targeting that can thwart even the most determined user. For the small business owner trying to squeeze marketing into the end of a long day, it's an overwhelming task that gets postponed indefinitely.

Brevi's vision was to level the playing field by providing an easy wizard for ad creation. Brevi would charge a flat rate and simplify the controls to guide the business owner from "Get Started" to "Launch My Ad" in minutes.

Brevi's vision was to level the playing field by making it easy to create an ad. The software would tap third-party data to suggest ad content tailored to the business. Brevi would charge a flat rate and simplify the controls so the business owner could launch their ad as quickly as possible.

Solution

We built a wizard to guide the user through the process of creating an ad. The wizard draws on business information such as the catalog for an e-commerce store or the scheduling system for a gym. Armed with this data, the wizard asks easy-to-answer questions to generate an ad promoting the business. After customizing the ad and paying with a credit card, the user receives email updates and can return to the site to track the progress of their campaign.

As technical co-founder, Aron developed the system including six partner OAuth and API integrations. Each integration offers access to a pool of users and the opportunity to test the market for ads in a given vertical. Aron also wrote a custom Facebook Graph client for batching requests, enabling back office reporting at scale.
