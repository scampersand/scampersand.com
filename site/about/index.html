How we do it

Over that time we've developed some shared philosophies that guide our collaboration:

  1. We invest in the up-front planning so that we can execute smoothly with a common understanding of the next steps and end product. Planning and execution are separate modes--if something comes up that calls for a change, we halt execution and toggle as a team to reassess the plan. We firmly believe that a disciplined process is the best way to avoid waste and frustration, and yields a better result.

  2. We investigate alternatives even when the answer seems obvious. The human tendency is to adopt the first solution we think of. By forcing ourselves to consider alternatives, we often discover solutions that are better and cost less.

  3. We enjoy our work. Doesn't mean there's never any stress, but we try to keep perspective, sigh and grin, and embrace the journey.

Who We Are

Our first experience working together was as college students--we were already pair programming when the term was first coined. Aron was preparing to debug a messy chain of conditionals when Amy said, "Don't do that, you know how it should work and it'll take less time to rewrite it from scratch."

The synergy we discovered in that moment became a constant, even as our careers alternately diverged and converged. We spent a while working on software together at HP, then Amy took a few years to pursue graphic design at RISD while Aron freelanced for a startup. We find ourselves intersecting again on web app design and implementation, and we're excited by the opportunity to partner as Scampersand.

Aron loves Python and Django, OAuth and APIs, 12-factor apps and ultimate frisbee.
